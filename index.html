Walmart Sales Forecasting: Time Series Analysis
Python

The Challenge: Retail forecasting faces a critical question: should we forecast at granular department levels and aggregate up, or aggregate sales first and then disaggregate predictions? Traditional wisdom suggests granular forecasting captures department-specific patterns, but this may amplify noise in volatile retail data.

My Approach: We developed a systematic comparison of two hierarchical forecasting strategies using Holt-Winters Exponential Smoothing on Walmart sales data. Method 1 forecasted directly at department level, while Method 2 aggregated to store level, forecasted, then disaggregated using historical proportions. Rigorous statistical testing (ADF, KPSS, Mann-Kendall) informed model selection, and grid search optimized smoothing parameters.

Key Results: Surprisingly, Method 2 (aggregate-disaggregate) outperformed Method 1 by 3.96% in RMSE for Store 1, Department 1. The aggregation approach reduced noise by 15% while maintaining stable department proportions, proving that maximum granularity doesn't always yield superior forecasts when temporal patterns are noisy but proportions are stable.

Impact: This research provides actionable insights for retail supply chain optimization, demonstrating when aggregation strategies outperform granular approaches. The findings directly impact inventory management, supplier contract optimization, and forecast-driven rebate maximization strategies in retail analytics.
